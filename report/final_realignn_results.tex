\section{Scope}

This document summarizes the final result tables and plots for ReALIGNN, our reimplementation and target-specific improvement of the original local ALIGNN baseline.  The final result set includes formation energy and the four follow-up targets:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{formation\_energy\_peratom}
  \item \texttt{optb88vdw\_bandgap}
  \item \texttt{ehull}
  \item \texttt{mbj\_bandgap}
  \item \texttt{bulk\_modulus\_kv}
\end{itemize}

No additional training was required for this final figure-generation step.  The plots and tables were generated from the completed same-split comparison results documented in the target reports under \texttt{report/} and from the saved prediction artifacts under \texttt{results/logs/}.

\section{Interpretation}

ReALIGNN improves the original same-split baseline for formation energy, \texttt{optb88vdw\_bandgap}, \texttt{ehull}, and \texttt{mbj\_bandgap} across MAE, RMSE, and P95 absolute error.  For \texttt{bulk\_modulus\_kv}, the best strict single model improves MAE and P95 error but has slightly worse RMSE due to a small number of hard high-error structures.  The validation-selected ensemble improves bulk-modulus MAE, RMSE, and P95 simultaneously, but is reported separately because it combines multiple trained ReALIGNN checkpoints.
